Welcome to Japan

Heritage & Background

Japan is an archipelago of over 14,000 islands (main four: Hokkaido, Honshu, Shikoku, Kyushu) with a land area of 378,000 km². Its people are primarily Yamato Japanese, descending from ancient Jōmon hunter-gatherers (14,000 BCE) and Yayoi rice farmers. Japan was never colonized and rapidly modernized after the 1868 Meiji Restoration. Today it is the world’s third-largest economy and a global leader in technology, automobiles, robotics, and high-speed rail. The official language is Japanese. Climate features four distinct seasons, two rainy periods, and occasional typhoons. Ethnic makeup is 98% Japanese with a strong emphasis on group harmony (wa) rooted in Shinto and Buddhism.

High Culture

Noh & Kabuki Theater

14th-century masked drama and vibrant stylized performances with live music — UNESCO treasures.

Ukiyo-e & Haiku

Hokusai’s Great Wave woodblock prints and Bashō’s timeless poetry capture fleeting beauty.

Kaiseki & Museums

Multi-course seasonal fine dining and the Tokyo National Museum housing 120,000+ national treasures.

Pop Culture

Anime & Manga

Studio Ghibli films, Pokémon, One Piece — Japan’s soft power reaches every continent.

J-Pop & Sports

  • Idol groups and global collaborations
  • NPB professional baseball
  • Harajuku street fashion & cosplay

Blockbusters & Celebrities

Fast-food chains like Yoshinoya alongside Michelin-starred ramen and worldwide anime stars.

Folk Culture

Matsuri Festivals

Gion Matsuri (over 1,000 years old) and hanami cherry-blossom viewing parties.

Taiko & Origami

Powerful drumming, delicate paper folding, Shinto shrine rituals, and ancient kami spirit legends.

Oshogatsu & Rites

New Year shrine visits, coming-of-age ceremonies, and regional harvest festivals.

Norms That May Surprise Visitors

Bowing & Shoes

Deep bow shows respect; remove shoes indoors — even in restaurants and homes.

Slurping & No Tipping

Loud noodle slurping is a compliment to the chef; tipping is considered rude.

Onsen Etiquette

Wash thoroughly before entering communal hot springs (nude, same-gender areas).

Japan Invites You

From Mount Fuji to neon Tokyo, every season and custom welcomes you with omotenashi — heartfelt hospitality. Come for the culture. Stay for memories that last a lifetime.
